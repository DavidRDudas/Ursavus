A company with a deep origin.

Drag through twenty million years — Miocene to now.

The bear we are named for is not the biggest or the fiercest. Ursavus — the dawn bear — was a small, fox-sized animal that lived around twenty million years ago. It was the first undisputed bear, and the common ancestor that grizzlies, pandas, and polar bears all descend from.

It earned that place by getting the form right, once. Everything afterward inherited it. That is exactly the kind of company we want to be: small and early, the careful origin that good software descends from.

01

Built to stay small.

Identifying field marks.

Ursavus is run by the people who build the product. We are not chasing a category or a headcount — small is a capability. It lets us say no, keep our promises, and answer our own mail.

i

Independence.

Founder-led, and answerable to the people who use our software — not to a board, a category, or a growth curve.

ii

Restraint.

We would rather ship one considered feature than three loud ones. The hard part is deciding what not to build.

iii

Custody.

Customer data is a responsibility, not an asset. Isolation is the default in everything we build.

iv

Craft.

Software is engineering and art at once — measured, fast, and correct, but also a pleasure to use. We refuse to choose.

What we build.

Two products, held to one standard.

Hokku

Service-operations platform

Service operations, taken seriously.

A service-desk and support-operations platform for teams that run on tickets. Work lives in workspaces and queues, and every ticket carries a fully customizable schema — drag-and-drop fields, conditional logic, calculated values, and stable identities, so a rename never corrupts data. On a fast Rust backend, with each customer's data in its own isolated database.

Schema builder
Custom fields, conditional logic, and calculated values, with identities stable across renames.
Flow designer
Visual automation with event, schedule, and manual triggers, branching, and a dry-run mode.
Customer portal
A branded place for customers to submit and track requests, separated by role.
Isolated by architecture
One database per organization. Isolation is the default, not a tier.
